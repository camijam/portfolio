Unlocking Cross-Cultural Communication in UX design

Cross-cultural communication has become a focus of importance for different companies seeking to internationalize their products. In a globalized world, a message, a design, a product or a service can be interpreted in different ways. Through cross-cultural communication, we can be aware of these cultural differences and use them to our advantage. In this article you will find 4 unmissable tips to keep in mind when it comes to cross-cultural communication.

What is behind cross-cultural communication

The point of understanding a culture must go beyond simply understanding the language. This means that translating web content is not enough if it is not culturally adapted. As human beings, we undoubtedly identify with our collective belief systems. For example, the place where we are born, the way we speak or what we learned as children. All of this creates social codes unique to our country or our group that makes us identify and represent us as a unique community.

Product design using cross-cultural communication

Cross-cultural design has a huge importance in the perception of users. Colors, symbols or the organization of the content have a great influence on the user experience and understanding of our message. For example, in web design, the way content is presented to users in the United States may not be the same as it is to users in China. A proper understanding of culture will make us understand that we perceive differently and to achieve a good interaction with our users we need to understand them first.

Meaning of colors

Colors are a very big topic in cross-cultural communication. Thus, the same color can have a different meaning according to the culture. It is interesting and at the same time it is necessary to be careful as this can completely impact the message we want to share. You should always keep in mind how to choose the right color combination and the best way is through research.

Additionally, some techniques for blending user experience-centered design and color can be the key of our communication strategy. At the end of the day, the combination of psychology, design and understanding of culture has yielded great results in creating communications and designs that impact all cultures.

When cross-cultural communication is not in the scope

Disregarding culture and employing a cross-cultural communication strategy has terrible consequences. When a company embarks on the internationalization of a product or service, the capital investment is enormous and there are many cases of monetary losses. For example, in 1950 Pepsi lost the Asian market by changing the color of its product to light blue, the meaning of which in this continent is related to death. This reinforces the need for proper research before launching a campaign in another culture.

Conclusion

In conclusion, in this article we address several issues that are important in cross-cultural communication. On the one hand, the importance behind cross-cultural communication. The power and importance of UX design and, additionally, the added value of colors and their meaning in different cultures. Finally, we highlighted the cases in which culture was not taken into account and the terrible effects for a brand. However, there are many more topics, we invite you to continue exploring cross-cultural communication and become more aware of this subject that we are all passionate about.
